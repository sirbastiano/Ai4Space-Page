AI4Space Workshop Overview

Advancing artificial intelligence for autonomous spacecraft, Earth observation, and next-generation space missions.

The AI4Space workshop at CVPR 2026 brings together researchers, engineers, and space domain experts to address the unique challenges of deploying AI in space environments. As missions demand greater autonomy, resilience, and on-board intelligence, this workshop explores how computer vision and machine learning can enable breakthrough capabilities in spacecraft navigation, planetary exploration, satellite data analysis, and orbital operations.

Key challenges we address: How can AI models generalize to harsh, data-constrained space environments? How do we design verifiable, fail-safe systems for mission-critical applications? How do we enable autonomous decision-making when ground control is hours or days away?

AI4Space fosters collaboration between machine learning researchers and space scientists, focusing on topics such as vision-based navigation, onboard learning, sim-to-real transfer for robotics, multi-sensor fusion for Earth observation, and AI-driven mission planningâ€”shaping the future of intelligent space exploration.
